Bouillabaisse

Looks Good, Don't It?

A rapidly cooked, but deeply flavored seafood broth

With an aromatic base and a multitude of fresh, robust flavors, this carefully simmered dish will be sure to leave you coming back for more

What You'll Need

  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1 onion, cut into small cubes
  • 1 carrot, finelychopped
  • 6 garlic cloves
  • 1 leek
  • 1 sea scallop
  • 6 raw shrimp, like really raw
  • 3 thyme sprigs
  • toasted baguette slices
  • rouille
  • white wine
  • piment d'Espelette

How to Make the Broth

  1. Melt butter, add onion, carrot, celery and leeks then cook until softened. Add tomato paste and stir
  2. Add tomato, shrimp, snaper and scallop then stir. Add thyme and d'Espelette. Add white wine and bring toboil, stir in saffron and add 4 cups water. Reduce heat to low and simmer 20 min
  3. Transfer seafood broth to a blender, secure lid and remove center piece to allow steam to escape
  4. Pour seafood broth through a fine wire-mesh strainer set over a large heatproof bowl, discard solids then set aside

How to Make the Bouillabaisse

  1. Combine the broth, orange zest and lemon zest in a large enameled cast iron casserole
  2. Add red snapper fillet, cook until cooked through, then add scallops and shrimp and cook until they are cooked through as well
  3. Remove from heat, season then divide the bouillabaisse among 4 serving bowls. Garnish with fennel fronds, chopped parseley and extra virgin olive oil and serve with toasted baguette slices and rouille
