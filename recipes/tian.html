Ever Heard of Ratatouille?

A Provencal-style dish with an air of summer simplicity

Generous amounts of parmesean mixed with soft, fresh vegetables give this dish its iconic history

What You'll Need

  • 3 small tomatoes
  • 2 yellow squash (arge)
  • 1 large zucchini
  • 1 small eggplant
  • 1 tspn salt (Kosher, divided)
  • 6 tbspn extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 grated garlic clove
  • 2 oz Parmesan cheese

How to Make

  1. Preheat oven to 400 degrees Farenheit. Spread tomato, squash, zucchini and eggplant slices onto a pan and sprinkle evenly with 3/8 tspn of salt
  2. Arrange stacked slices of vegetables neatly and upright into a lightly greates 2 1/2 quart baking dish. Sprinkle with peper
  3. Whisk together oil and garlic, drizzle evenly. Sprinkle Parmesan and bake in prehated over until cheese is golden and vegetables are tender and charred. Roughly 25 minutes
  4. Scoop onto a serving dish along with your favorite choice of protein and enjoy!
